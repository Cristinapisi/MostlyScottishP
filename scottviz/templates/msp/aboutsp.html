The current Parliament exists since May 12, 1999. This is the fourth session and it will last for 5 years (until the 5 may 2016 elections). All previous sessions have been 4 years long (1999-2003, 2003-2007, 2007-2011).

There are (usually) 129 Members of the Scottish Parliament, reffered to, in short, as MSPs. Out of the 129 members, 73 represent individual geographical constituencies and 56 are elected as 7 for each of the 8. Each person in Scotland is therefore represented by 8: 1 for his constituency and 7 for his region. To find the MSPs that represents you, enter your full postcode in the search bar at the top right corner.

The MSPs vote, several times a week, on devolved matters. At the moment these include:
  • agriculture, forestry and fisheries
  • education and training
  • environment
  • health and social services
  • housing
  • law and order
  • local government
  • sport and the arts
  • tourism and economic development
  • many aspects of transport
