What extensions/changes we have in mind?

We realise that we cannot provide all the visualisations users might want. Therefore, we have designed the system such that it is easy for you to add your own algorithm for scatter plotting and it will render the data visually. All it needs is 2-dimensional data!

We know that the format in which the Scottish Parliament makes the divisions available can change (it did before, and none of the other similar sites managed to cope with it effectively). We have therefore isolated the script that retrieves pages, the one that reads those and the one that populates the database from each other and from the rest of the system (read about modular programming). If anything changes at the Scottish Parliament side, some/all of the scripts mentioned earlier will need to change.

The topics chosen for divisions and the weightings associated, are based more on our personal experience and critical evaluation. There is little to no basis for why it was done this way and not another. But hey, most times it works! That said, it could be bettered.

At the moment we are only processing the current Parliamentary Session. We have put in place some means of extending it to several. For that we need:

  • different retrievers and scrapers
  • slightly change the database schema
  • slightly change populating calls
  • add filter by session for analytics if sessions dealt with separately

As far as we know, most of the information is processed correctly. However, there are some assumptions and some information we are not taking full advantage off. You could therefore try to fine tune analytics.

  • At the moment, we are not treating differently MSPs that were members for a shorter time (reasons: deceased, resigned, joined to replace).
    The Presiding Officer, who cannot actually vote is not treat differently either (assumption: absent for all divisions).
    As a consequence, there weren't always exactly 129 MSP members. For example, now there are 128, one of which is the Presiding Officer.
    This could slightly affect rebellion, attendance rates and the scatter plot.

  • At the moment we are not processing the dates for divisions to see trends for attendance through the week, year and even entire Parliament session. We would expect at least some MSPs to be present more towards the beginning and the end, so as to make a good impression on electors.
    Analogous, we could look for trends in rebellions across topics, the year or the Parliament session. We would expect at least some MSPs to be less rebellious towards the beginning and the end, to get support from their party.
